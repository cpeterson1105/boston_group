Electric Vehicles Reduce Carbon Dioxide Emissions

On average, traditional gas-powered cars emit approximately 400 grams of carbon dioxide per mile. Emissions contribute to climate change in addition to negatively impacting air quality. Because electric vehicles do not use internal combustion, they produce no tailpipe exhaust. This ensures electric vehicles' net carbon dioxide emissions are considerably less.

Carbon dioxide levels in the Earth's atmosphere have risen 43% since the Industrial Revolution. Carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases trap heat in the atmosphere, resulting in a global temperature increase of more than 2 degrees Fahrenheit−a change large enough to melt polar ice caps, contribute to massive forest fires, and cause extreme weather patterns. Personal vehicles accocunt for roughly one-fifth of greenhouse gas emissions in the United States.

Electric Vehicles Poised for Growth In Boston

Assuming local growth mirrors global trends, Boston will have 59,000 electric vehicles by the end of 2030.

According to Go Boston 2030 there were only 351 electric vehicles in Boston in 2014. However, the International Energy Agency's Global EV Outlook of 2019 predicts considerable growth in the number of EVs to more than 150 times the 2015 stock. If Boston experiences that level of expansion, which given Boston's resources is conservative, EVs in the city will exceed 59,000 in 2030.

Electric Vehicles Will Reduce Boston's Carbon Dioxide Emissions

Assuming IEA's EV30@30 Scenario, EV adoption would achieve approximately 50,000 tons of annual reduction in CO2 emissions in 2030−the equivalent of preventing the deforestation of nearly 2 million trees.

The IEA predicts that worldwide EVs will reduce 6% of CO2 emissions in the transportation sector by 2030. Based on the Massachusetts vehicle census from 2014, there were nearly 260,000 households in Boston producing on average 3.57 Mt CO2-eq from annual driving or nearly 930,000 Mt CO2 -eq for the entire city of Boston. Based on the IEA projections of EV adoption noted above, total emissions in 2030 will drop by 52,607 Mt CO2-eq. Under the "Recharge Boston" initiative, we expect that the City of Boston can surpass these projections, leading to further CO2 reduction. Given the ambitious target set by the Climate Action Plan, the city must proactively remove obstacles to the introduction of EVs.
